How to Play Chess

  1. Setup: The game is played on an 8x8 board with alternating colors of squares. Each player starts with 16 pieces: 8 pawns, 2 rooks, 2 knights, 2 bishops, 1 queen, and 1 king.
  2. Objective: The objective of chess is to checkmate your opponent's king, meaning their king is in a position where it can be captured and no legal moves can avoid this.
  3. Basic Moves:
    • Pawns: Move forward one square (or two squares on their first move). Pawns capture diagonally.
    • Rooks: Move any number of squares vertically or horizontally.
    • Knights: Move in an "L" shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular.
    • Bishops: Move diagonally across the board.
    • Queens: Move any number of squares vertically, horizontally, or diagonally.
    • Kings: Move one square in any direction.
  4. Special Moves:
    • Castling: The king and one of the rooks move simultaneously, provided neither piece has moved before and there are no pieces between them.
    • En Passant: A special pawn capture that can occur immediately after an opponent moves their pawn two squares forward.
    • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the far end of the board, it is promoted to any piece except a king (usually a queen).
  5. Endgame: The game ends when one player checkmates the other player's king. A game can also end in a draw under certain conditions.
